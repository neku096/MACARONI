Terms and Commercial Use

Where should I check the usage terms?

Please check this site's Terms. Item-specific BOOTH conditions are grouped in the BOOTH terms tab on the Terms page.

Can I use the product commercially?

Some products can be used for videos, avatar photography, or game production, but redistribution, reselling the material itself, or packaging the raw material for others may be prohibited. Always check the product page and terms.

Troubleshooting

EXMenu does not work correctly after uploading the avatar.

If EXMenu does not appear, does not respond, or does not switch correctly after uploading the avatar, first check whether Modular Avatar is installed. If it is missing, EXMenu and parameters may not be applied correctly.

Even when Modular Avatar is installed, the following can still cause issues.

  • Required objects are not placed directly under the avatar
  • MAMST_Controller is not placed in the correct position
  • The prefab was placed in the wrong location
  • Modular Avatar settings were not applied correctly

For detailed checks, see the MAMST_Controller setup notes.

Hair or accessories jump or float during motion playback.

On some avatars, hair, clothes, or accessories can react strongly during motion playback because of PhysBone settings, causing them to jump or float unexpectedly.

The animation files included with these products generally do not directly key hair bones. If hair spreads, jumps, or floats, the following avatar-side settings may be involved.

  • VRC PhysBone on hair
  • PhysBone Gravity
  • VRC PhysBone Colliders around the hair or head
  • Interference from Constraints or Colliders
  • Avatar-specific bone structure or physics settings

Try lowering the hair PhysBone Gravity, reducing Collider Radius, moving Colliders away from areas that interfere, or temporarily weakening hair PhysBone settings. The result can vary by avatar because it depends on each avatar's physics setup.

Before Contacting

  • You checked the included notes and usage terms
  • You can describe the product name, avatar name, Unity version, and the issue

When contacting me, include the product name, avatar, Unity version, issue description, and screenshots if useful. Hide unnecessary details if personal information appears in a screenshot.
